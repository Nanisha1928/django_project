A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (GHGs) released into the atmosphere by an individual, business, or product. It's a measure of how much a person or entity contributes to climate change.

What's included in a carbon footprint? Activities: Driving, using electricity, eating food, and manufacturing products Gases: Carbon dioxide (\(CO_{2}\)), methane (\(CH_{4}\)), and nitrous oxide (\(N_{2}O\))

How's it measured? Carbon footprints are measured in units of carbon dioxide equivalents (\(CO_{2}e\)) Carbon footprint calculators estimate emissions based on lifestyle or business data.

Who can have a carbon footprint? Individuals, Products, and Businesses.

How can it be reduced? Use clean energy sources, Reduce consumption, Recycle and reuse, Improve energy efficiency, and Invest in environmental projects.
